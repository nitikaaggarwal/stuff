  • Dr. Christoph Michel, Senior Advisor

    Dr. Michel is a Professor for Clinical Neuroscience at the Medical Faculty of the University of Geneva. He is head of the Functional Brain Mapping Laboratory at the Neuroscience Department of the University of Geneva and Director of the EEG core of the Lemanic Biomedical Imaging Center. He has successfully conducted research on cognitive enhancement for over 25 years and published his work on the effects that Piracetam has on brain function in healthy individuals. Dr. Michel is Chief Editor of the Journal Brain Topography, a member of the Board of the Swiss League against Epilepsy, and co-author of the book Electrical Neuroimaging (Cambridge University Press). He studied Biology at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich and received his PhD in Behavioral Biology in 1988 with a thesis on EEG correlates of human information processing and psychopharmacological influences.

    Dr. Andrew Hill, Lead Neuroscientist

    Dr. Hill received his PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience from UCLA in 2012, studying how attention operates in the brain. He is currently lecturing for the Undergraduate Education Initiatives program at UCLA, teaching a course sequence gerontology, and the neuroscience of healthy brain aging. Dr. Hill has published chapters on measuring and modulating human attention, and continues to research self regulation. Prior to UCLA, Dr. Hill obtained extensive experience working with both psychiatric and developmental populations as well as gaining experience in high technology areas. He received his B.S. in Psychology/Neuroscience from UMass Amherst. See Andrew’s book chapters Measuring & Modulating Hemispheric Attention and EEG Correlates of Hemispheric Word Recognition.

    Christopher Thompson, Founder & CEO

    Chris founded truBrain to help overworked professionals maximize cognitive performance with a formula made by the world’s top neuroscientists. Prior to truBrain, Chris had a direct operating role in brand management at Unilever and managed consulting engagements for F500 consumer products clients including Wrigley’s, Nokia, and Pfizer. Chris has an MBA from Duke and a BA in Economics from Georgetown.
